# **1\. INTRODUCTION** {#1.-introduction}

## **1.1. About *FIRST*® Global** {#1.1.-about-first®-global}

*FIRST*® Global organizes a yearly international robotics challenge to ignite a passion for science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) among the more than two billion youths across the world. By bringing these future STEM leaders together in an engaging and collaborative competition that drives home the importance, excitement, and applicability of STEM education, *FIRST* Global inspires students to learn the skills they will need to make the discoveries their parents and grandparents would consider miracles, impossibilities, or just plain science fiction.

*FIRST* Global also strives to convince the various governments and organizations of the world to embrace science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) education, and to support it by investing in their young adults that will soon begin to make their marks in the world.

Themed around the greatest challenges facing our planet, each year a different challenge takes center stage in an effort to foster understanding and cooperation among the youth of the world as they use their abilities to solve the world’s problems.

Participation in *FIRST* Global is about more than just robots. As participants learn to find solutions to the world’s greatest challenges – water, energy, security, medicine, food, climate, and education – they learn how to work with each other, trust each other, and become part of a truly global community.

More information about *FIRST* Global, including contact information and links to social media, can be found on the [*FIRST* Global](https://first.global/) website.

## **1.2. The 2026 *FIRST* Global Challenge – *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge** {#1.2.-the-2026-first-global-challenge-–-igniting-innovation-robotics-challenge}

Fire has shaped our planet for millennia, but intensifying climate change has turned wildfires into a planetary crisis burning through landscapes, releasing carbon, and accelerating the next disaster. The 2026 theme of *Igniting Innovation* challenges *FIRST* Global teams to break this cycle, using technology to respond, recover, and prevent wildfires while safeguarding our ecosystems and communities worldwide.

The [*Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge](https://youtu.be/F9NJbtEffco?si=-OpytjC0Yj23DXzA) focuses specifically on the importance of stopping the spread of existing wildfires and preventing future wildfires.

# **2\. THE GAME** {#2.-the-game}

## **2.1. Game Description** {#2.1.-game-description}

The *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge is played on a 7 m by 7 m elevated platform. Six teams compete with their ROBOTS on the PLAYING FIELD during the 2 minutes and 30 seconds (2:30) of play, with each MATCH featuring a different randomized combination of teams.

The objective of the *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge is for two REGIONAL ALLIANCES, each composed of three teams, to stop the spread of existing wildfires by CONTAINING WILDFIRE in the SUPPRESSION UNITS using ROBOTS. At the same time, all six teams work as a single GLOBAL ALLIANCE to CONTAIN WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER using HUMAN PLAYERS. REGIONAL ALLIANCES then CLIMB the BRACES to prevent future wildfires. Teams that prioritize working together to accelerate solutions earn additional points for PARTNER CLIMBS. If four or more ROBOTS unite on ZONE 3 of the BRACES to safeguard the planet from wildfires, all six teams receive additional points, known as a *COOPERTITION*® BONUS.

## **2.2. Playing Field** {#2.2.-playing-field}

The 7 m by 7 m PLAYING FIELD is elevated approximately 70 cm off the ground and is bounded by the GUARDRAILS, a system of borders at least 20 cm tall surrounding the PLAYING FIELD perimeter. The PLAYING FIELD is inclusive of the SUPPRESSION UNITS, EXTINGUISHER, BRACES, FIRE SHIELDS, and the inward facing surfaces of the GUARDRAILS. The PLAYING FIELD SURFACE is the horizontal surface bounded by the GUARDRAILS and covered with a low pile carpet similar to those found in many public or commercial buildings. Any low pile carpet may be used for practice and will react in a similar way for GAME PIECES and ROBOTS.

Red and blue ALLIANCE STATIONS, located on opposing sides of the PLAYING FIELD, are where the DRIVE TEAMS (DRIVERS, CAPTAINS, and HUMAN PLAYERS) stand during the MATCH. The team makeup of the REGIONAL ALLIANCES in each MATCH is randomly assigned during the 2026 *FIRST* Global Challenge in Incheon, Republic of Korea (referred to as the EVENT). The specific location for each DRIVE TEAM within the ALLIANCE STATIONS is determined by the REGIONAL ALLIANCE members. DRIVE TEAMS must remain within the boundaries of the ALLIANCE STATION, which is marked on the floor with gaffer tape and extends outward 150 cm from the edge of the GUARDRAIL.

The HUMAN PLAYER ZONE is an area within each ALLIANCE STATION near the FIRE SHIELDS and marked with gaffer tape on the floor. HUMAN PLAYERS must be standing in the HUMAN PLAYER ZONE when CONTAINING WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER or interacting with the FIRE SHIELD. Other DRIVE TEAM members are not allowed to enter the HUMAN PLAYER ZONE.

The REGIONAL ZONES are parallel to the ALLIANCE STATIONS and adjacent to the GUARDRAILS. They are 410 cm long and extend 50 cm from the GUARDRAIL and are marked with gaffer tape on the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE.

PLAYING FIELD drawings and CAD models can be found at [https://first.global/fgc/](https://first.global/fgc/#game).

**![][image3]**

**Figure 2-2: *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge PLAYING FIELD Layout**

The SUPPRESSION UNITS are 201 cm-tall polycarbonate structures located on either side of the EXTINGUISHER, one red and one blue for each REGIONAL ALLIANCE. Each SUPPRESSION UNIT has a canopy at the top that projects over an opening. The height of the bottom edge of the canopy is 165 cm, and it extends outward 40 cm from the vertical surface of the SUPPRESSION UNIT. The door on the front face of each SUPPRESSION UNIT is for field reset use only. A slanting internal incline within each SUPPRESSION UNIT directs WILDFIRE into the front-facing containment area. There are vertical LED indicators within each SUPPRESSION UNIT to indicate the level of WILDFIRE, as well as the status of the PLAYING FIELD.

**![][image4]         ![][image5]**

**Figure 2-3: SUPPRESSION UNITS**

The EXTINGUISHER, located between the two SUPPRESSION UNITS, is a polycarbonate structure with a 201 cm-tall backboard. The opening in the front is 166 cm tall, with a ledge that extends 40 cm from the vertical surface of the EXTINGUISHER. The door on the front face of the EXTINGUISHER is for field reset use only. The space beneath the EXTINGUISHER, an opening 28 cm tall, is for WILDFIRE to enter the PLAYING FIELD at the start of the MATCH. There are vertical LED indicators within each EXTINGUISHER to indicate the level of WILDFIRE, as well as the status of the PLAYING FIELD. The EXTINGUISHER is shared by the two REGIONAL ALLIANCES.

**![][image6]**

**Figure 2-4: EXTINGUISHER**

Two polycarbonate FIRE SHIELDS, colored red and blue for each REGIONAL ALLIANCE, are located at the corners of the PLAYING FIELD adjacent to each HUMAN PLAYER ZONE. Each FIRE SHIELD has a clear polycarbonate panel on the side and an opening at the bottom, called the PORT, into which ROBOTS can push WILDFIRE. There is an angled CHUTE located on top of the FIRE SHIELD to transfer WILDFIRE to ROBOTS and the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE. A GATE at the lower end of the CHUTE opens when a HUMAN PLAYER actuates a LEVER.

**![][image7]**

**Figure 2-5: FIRE SHIELD**

The BRACES are mounted to the GUARDRAIL near each FIRE SHIELD and extend up to the top outer corners of the EXTINGUISHER. They are made of 6.4 m-long galvanized steel pipe covered with red and blue heat shrink for each REGIONAL ALLIANCE. The height at the low end is 24 cm, and the height at the tall end is 197 cm, with maximum deflection at the center of 100 mm. Each BRACE is divided into three ZONES (ZONE 1, ZONE 2, and ZONE 3), marked by gaffer tape. The BRACES are connected to the PLAYING FIELD by support hardware on the two ends.

# **3\. HOW TO PLAY AND SCORING** {#3.-how-to-play-and-scoring}

## **3.1. Overview** {#3.1.-overview}

There are two elements to each MATCH. During the REGIONAL ALLIANCE element, teams work in groups of three, a REGIONAL ALLIANCE, to CONTAIN WILDFIRE in the SUPPRESSION UNITS, then CLIMB the BRACE. During the GLOBAL ALLIANCE element, all six teams work together to CONTAIN WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER.

| Wildfires are uncontrolled fires that can start naturally, such as from lightning, or through human activities like unattended campfires. Fueled by dry vegetation, wind, and heat, they can spread rapidly and threaten ecosystems, wildlife, homes, and human lives. While wildfires can damage air quality and release large amounts of carbon dioxide, some ecosystems rely on periodic fires to support new plant growth and recycle nutrients back into the soil. As climate change increases drought and extreme temperatures, wildfires are becoming more frequent and severe, making mitigation increasingly important. Wildfire mitigation focuses on both preventing wildfires before they begin and reducing their intensity and spread once they occur. |
| :---- |

## **3.2. Regional Alliance Element** {#3.2.-regional-alliance-element}

DRIVE TEAMS start the MATCH standing in their ALLIANCE STATION. Teams are grouped into two REGIONAL ALLIANCES, made of three randomly assigned teams each, one designated red and the other blue.

| A geographical region approach to wildfire mitigation adapts prevention and management strategies to the specific climate, vegetation, terrain, and fire risks of an area. Different regions require different methods, such as controlled burns in forests or firebreaks in grasslands, while cooperation between local governments, scientists, firefighters, and communities helps create effective wildfire plans. This is represented by the REGIONAL ALLIANCE element of each MATCH. |
| :---- |

ROBOTS start the MATCH in the REGIONAL ZONE next to their corresponding ALLIANCE STATION, in contact with the GUARDRAIL. The MATCH begins when the announcer says “go.”

When the MATCH starts, 500 WILDFIRE are released onto the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE from beneath the EXTINGUISHER.

The ROBOTS on each REGIONAL ALLIANCE work to CONTAIN WILDFIRE in the SUPPRESSION UNIT designated with their REGIONAL ALLIANCE color (red or blue).

This can be accomplished in two ways:

* ROBOTS collect WILDFIRE from the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE and directly CONTAIN it in the SUPPRESSION UNIT.
* ROBOTS maneuver WILDFIRE from the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE into the FIRE SHIELD PORTS. Once WILDFIRE is inside the FIRE SHIELD, the HUMAN PLAYER removes WILDFIRE, places it in the CHUTE, and actuates the LEVER to open the GATE. Then the ROBOT CONTAINS WILDFIRE in the SUPPRESSION UNIT.

A REGIONAL ALLIANCE earns points for each WILDFIRE CONTAINED in the SUPPRESSION UNIT at a rate of 1 point per WILDFIRE. WILDFIRE may be CONTAINED in the SUPPRESSION UNITS at any point during the MATCH.

| In the *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge, WILDFIRE quickly spreads across the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE. Teams work to CONTAIN WILDFIRE, representing the creation of barriers that prevent wildfire from spreading beyond a controlled area. Fire lines, cleared vegetation, controlled burns, and natural barriers such as rivers or roads are used to surround the fire and limit its movement.  The SUPPRESSION UNITS symbolize the tools and techniques that can slow the fire’s advance, such as water drops from aircraft, fire retardants, hoses, bulldozers, and hand crews that remove vegetation. FIRE SHIELDS help protect the HUMAN PLAYERS as they take action to gain control of WILDFIRE. |
| :---- |

Towards the end of the MATCH, each REGIONAL ALLIANCE ROBOT CLIMBS the BRACE to earn a CLIMB MULTIPLIER. Each BRACE is divided into three ZONES, and the CLIMB MULTIPLIER value is determined by ROBOT position on the BRACE. ROBOTS must begin CLIMBING the BRACE in ZONE 1 and can only traverse into ZONES 2 and 3 by starting the CLIMB from ZONE 1\. The tape line defining each ZONE is considered the upper limit of the lower ZONE.

There are four levels of increasing CLIMB MULTIPLIER value:

* CONTACT
  * ROBOT is in contact with ZONE 1 and remains in contact with the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE at the end of the MATCH
  * MULTIPLIER increment: 0.05 per ROBOT
* ZONE 1
  * ROBOT is fully supported (directly or indirectly) by ZONE 1 of the BRACE and is not in contact with the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE
  * MULTIPLIER increment: 0.10 per ROBOT
* ZONE 2
  * ROBOT is fully supported (directly or indirectly) by ZONE 2 of the BRACE and is not in contact with the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE
  * MULTIPLIER increment: 0.20 per ROBOT
* ZONE 3
  * ROBOT is fully supported (directly or indirectly) by ZONE 3 of the BRACE and is not in contact with the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE
  * MULTIPLIER increment: 0.30 per ROBOT

**![][image11]**

**Figure 3-1: BRACE ZONES**

A ROBOT that is “fully supported indirectly” means the full weight of the ROBOT is supported by the BRACE in a specific ZONE, even if the ROBOT is not in direct contact with the BRACE itself. For example, if a ROBOT’S weight is transferred to the BRACE through direct contact with another ROBOT’S mechanism.

The sum of each REGIONAL ALLIANCE’s CLIMB MULTIPLIER is applied to the REGIONAL ALLIANCE’s SUPPRESSION UNIT points. All fractional scores round up to the nearest whole number.

| Prevention efforts are critical to preventing future wildfires. In each MATCH, this is illustrated as ROBOTS CLIMB the BRACES. The progressively higher ZONES of each BRACE are more difficult to achieve but result in a greater impact on wildfire prevention, both now and in the future: CONTACT represents educating ourselves and others about wildfire risks, safe fire practices, evacuation planning, and ways to prevent human-caused fires. ZONE 1 represents tracking weather and fuel conditions to better predict wildfires. ZONE 2 represents using advanced technology, such as satellites, drones, and AI-powered cameras, to detect, predict, and reduce wildfire risks. ZONE 3 represents developing new technologies, tools, and strategies to better prepare for the future and build long-term resilience against increasingly severe wildfires. |
| :---- |

A REGIONAL ALLIANCE can earn additional PARTNER CLIMB points if a ROBOT that has CLIMBED the BRACE supports one or both of the REGIONAL ALLIANCE partner ROBOTS. Each ROBOT fully supported by another ROBOT earns 25 points.

| Regions helping each other with wildfire prevention is an important strategy for accelerating solutions and managing large and increasingly severe wildfires. This cooperation, represented by the PARTNER CLIMB, allows regions with greater resources or lower fire activity to support areas facing dangerous wildfire conditions. |
| :---- |

## **3.3. Global Alliance Element** {#3.3.-global-alliance-element}

All six teams work together as a single GLOBAL ALLIANCE to CONTAIN WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER.

| A global approach to mitigating wildfires involves countries working together to manage and reduce the impacts of wildfires worldwide. This includes sharing resources, technology, scientific research, and emergency response strategies, and is represented by the GLOBAL ALLIANCE element of each MATCH. By collaborating across borders, countries can better protect ecosystems, communities, and the global climate from the growing threat of wildfires. |
| :---- |

The HUMAN PLAYERS on each REGIONAL ALLIANCE work to CONTAIN WILDFIRE by depositing it in the EXTINGUISHER.

This can be accomplished in one way:

* ROBOTS maneuver WILDFIRE from the PLAYING FIELD SURFACE into the FIRE SHIELD PORTS. Once WILDFIRE is inside the FIRE SHIELD, the HUMAN PLAYER removes WILDFIRE and directly CONTAINS WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER.
  * Note: Since CONTAINING WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER benefits the GLOBAL ALLIANCE, any ROBOT may maneuver WILDFIRE to either FIRE SHIELD.

All six teams on the GLOBAL ALLIANCE earn points for WILDFIRE CONTAINED in the EXTINGUISHER at a rate of 1 point per WILDFIRE. WILDFIRE may be CONTAINED in the EXTINGUISHER at any point during the MATCH.

| Extinguishing a fire means fully putting out the fire by removing heat, oxygen, or fuel. Firefighters use water, fire retardant, foam, and hand tools to cool hot spots and eliminate flames until no active fire remains. This is represented by HUMAN PLAYERS CONTAINING WILDFIRE in the EXTINGUISHER. |
| :---- |

## **3.4. *Coopertition*® Bonus** {#3.4.-coopertition®-bonus}

The GLOBAL ALLIANCE earns additional points, known as a *COOPERTITION*® BONUS, if four or more ROBOTS are fully supported (directly or indirectly) by ZONE 3 of the BRACES at the end of the MATCH.

* Four ROBOTS fully supported by ZONE 3 of the BRACE \= 10 *COOPERTITION* BONUS points
* Five ROBOTS fully supported by ZONE 3 of the BRACE \= 25 *COOPERTITION* BONUS points
* Six ROBOTS fully supported by ZONE 3 of the BRACE \= 40 *COOPERTITION* BONUS points

| If a majority of nations develop and implement wildfire prevention methods, the entire planet is rewarded. The *COOPERTITION* BONUS is a reminder that as nations unite to safeguard the planet from wildfires, benefits such as decreased damage and improved air quality, extend far beyond individual regions. |
| :---- |

## **4.2. Violations** {#4.2.-violations}

The consequences for violating rules are defined below.

**TABLE 4-1: VIOLATIONS**

| Violation Consequence | Details |
| :---- | :---- |
| WHITE CARD | An official designation assessed to a team for failing to come to a MATCH with at least a HUMAN PLAYER. In RANKING MATCHES, when a team is issued a WHITE CARD, the team receives 0 points for that specific MATCH. |
| YELLOW CARD | An official warning issued to a team for breaking a rule. Additional YELLOW CARDS may be elevated to a RED CARD at the judgment of the HEAD REFEREE, depending on the severity and frequency of the offense. |
| YELLOW CARD \+ MINOR FOUL | An official warning plus penalty assessed to a team for breaking a rule. YELLOW CARD \+ MINOR FOUL issued to teams during the MATCH comes with a point penalty equivalent to 5% of the offending REGIONAL ALLIANCE’S MATCH score. The penalty points will be awarded to the opposing REGIONAL ALLIANCE (rather than deducted from the offending REGIONAL ALLIANCE’S MATCH score). Additional YELLOW CARD \+ MINOR FOULS may be elevated to a RED CARD at the judgment of the HEAD REFEREE, depending on the severity and frequency of the offense. A MINOR FOUL point penalty is calculated from the pre-penalty MATCH score. Multiple MINOR FOULS assessed to a REGIONAL ALLIANCE during a single MATCH are cumulative. |
| YELLOW CARD \+ MAJOR FOUL | An official warning plus penalty assessed to a team for breaking a rule. YELLOW CARD \+ MAJOR FOUL issued to teams during the MATCH comes with a point penalty equivalent to 10% of the offending REGIONAL ALLIANCE’S MATCH score. The penalty points will be awarded to the opposing REGIONAL ALLIANCE (rather than deducted from the offending REGIONAL ALLIANCE’S MATCH score). Additional YELLOW CARD \+ MAJOR FOULS may be elevated to a RED CARD at the judgment of the HEAD REFEREE, depending on the severity and frequency of the offense. A MAJOR FOUL point penalty is calculated from the pre-penalty MATCH score. Multiple MAJOR FOULS assessed to a REGIONAL ALLIANCE during a single MATCH are cumulative. |
| RED CARD | An official penalty assessed to a team for repeatedly breaking a rule, breaking multiple rules, or egregious actions. Generally, a YELLOW CARD is assessed to a team before a RED CARD, but it is at the HEAD REFEREE’S judgment to issue a RED CARD based on the severity of the offense. In RANKING MATCHES, when a team is issued a RED CARD, the team receives 0 points for that specific MATCH. In PLAYOFF and FINAL MATCHES, when a team is issued a RED CARD, the full TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE receives 0 points for that specific MATCH. A RED CARD MATCH may not be considered as the lowest-scoring MATCH to be dropped from a team’s overall RANKING score. |
| DISQUALIFIED | A team is no longer allowed to participate in any aspect of the EVENT. |
| ROBOT disabled for MATCH | The offending ROBOT is disabled by the HEAD REFEREE during MATCH and will no longer be in play. The team with the disabled ROBOT will still earn points for the MATCH. |
| ROBOT not allowed to compete | The offending ROBOT is not allowed to participate in the MATCH, and the team receives no points for that MATCH. |
| Must be corrected before MATCH can start | The problem must be corrected before the MATCH can start, otherwise the ROBOT is not allowed to compete in the MATCH. |

# **5\. THE ROBOT** {#5.-the-robot}

## **5.1. Overview** {#5.1.-overview}

This section outlines the rules and requirements for the design and construction of a 2026 *FIRST* Global ROBOT. *FIRST* Global ROBOTS are teleoperated and designed and built by student team members to compete in the *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge. REV Robotics supplies the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts from which teams will build their ROBOTS. Spare or replacement parts, as well as additional information about materials contained in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts, can be found on the [REV Robotics](https://www.revrobotics.com/) website. ROBOT build guides, technical resources, and CAD files of the components included in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts can be found at [https://first.global/fgc/robot-kit/](https://first.global/fgc/robot-kit/).

## **5.2. Robot Design and Construction Rules** {#5.2.-robot-design-and-construction-rules}

**R01	Teams must build a ROBOT that follows all rules.** These rules are written in a simple manner to make them as accessible as possible. Attempts to gain competitive advantage by exploiting rule interpretation violates the rules and does not meet the spirit of *FIRST* Global. MATCHES will not be delayed if a team is in violation of this rule (see **M06** and **R09**).

*Violation Consequence: ROBOT not allowed to compete or Must be corrected before MATCH can start.*

**R02	Each *FIRST* Global team may only enter one ROBOT in the competition.** Changes and modifications may be made to the ROBOT throughout the EVENT, but there can only be one ROBOT per team. Teams may not bring additional ROBOTS or prebuilt ROBOT subsystems to give or lend to other teams.

*Violation Consequence: DISQUALIFIED.*

**R03	ROBOTS must function in imperfect situations.** The PLAYING FIELD and placement of components making up the PLAYING FIELD may vary by up to ± 25 mm except if specifically noted in the 2026 FGC Playing Field Drawings. ROBOT designs should be robust enough to work even if the PLAYING FIELD, GAME PIECES, are not perfectly sized or inflated or have a slightly different texture.

**R04**	**ROBOTS must be designed to start each MATCH within a 50 cm x 50 cm x 50 cm volume.** The ROBOT must be contained within a 50 cm x 50 cm x 50 cm volume at the start of the MATCH. ROBOTS that expand must be able to do so independently and may not have detachable parts (see **R07**).

**R05**	**ROBOTS may extend no more than 50 cm in one horizontal direction after the MATCH begins.** ROBOTS may only extend in only one horizontal direction (left, right, front, or back) a maximum of 50 cm. There is no limit to vertical extension.

![][image15]

**Figure 4-1: ROBOT Extension**

**R06 	There is no minimum or maximum weight restriction on the ROBOT.**

**R07	ROBOTS must remain in one piece on the PLAYING FIELD.** ROBOTS may not intentionally detach parts on the PLAYING FIELD during the MATCH.

*Violation Consequence: YELLOW CARD, YELLOW CARD \+ MINOR or MAJOR FOUL, RED CARD, or ROBOT disabled for MATCH depending on severity of offense.*

**R08	ROBOTS must be designed to permit easy removal of GAME PIECES without requiring the ROBOT to be powered, as well as removal of the ROBOT from the BRACE.** For example, the ROBOT should not require power to run a mechanism, actuate a servo, or reverse an intake to release a GAME PIECE or the mechanism attaching it to the BRACE.

**R09	Each ROBOT must pass an initial inspection before being allowed to play in any RANKING or PLAYOFF MATCH.**
A.	If a HEAD REFEREE or INSPECTOR determines that a ROBOT is in violation of any rules, the team may be issued a RED CARD for the current MATCH (if they are playing) and will be required to pass a re-inspection before competing in their next MATCH.
B.	ROBOTS may be re-inspected at any time.
C.	If a team makes major modifications to their ROBOT, re-inspection is required.

*Violation Consequence: ROBOT not allowed to compete or RED CARD depending on severity of offense.*

**R10	ROBOTS must be constructed from only the components and quantities included in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts unless otherwise explicitly allowed.** Each team will receive one 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts composed of a single cardboard shipping box containing a FTC Starter Kit tote and a box of *FIRST* Global-Specific Components.

The official list of allowable components is identified in the combined 2026 FGC Bill of Materials, with the following exceptions:
A.	Tools (5.5 mm nut driver, 5.5 mm combination wrenches, \#25 chain tool, and Allen wrenches).
B.	Control System (battery charger and REV USB PS4 Compatible Gamepad).
C.	FTC Starter Kit Guide and Bill of Materials.
D.	2026 *FIRST* Global Challenge GAME PIECES (WILDFIRE).
E.	PLAYING FIELD components (galvanized steel pipe, heat shrink)
G.	Plastic storage tote.
H.	Cardboard boxes.
I.	Plastic and foam packaging materials.

*Violation Consequence: ROBOT not allowed to compete.*

| The GAME PIECE manufacturer does not sell to the general public, therefore identical GAME PIECES to those provided in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts and used at the EVENT are not available for purchase directly. GAME PIECES will be available for purchase from REV Robotics at [https://revrobotics.global/](https://revrobotics.global/). CAD files of the components included in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts can be found at [https://first.global/fgc/robot-kit/](https://first.global/fgc/robot-kit/).  |

**R11	ROBOTS may only be constructed using official REV Robotics components from the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts.** Unless otherwise specified in **R12**, no substitutions are allowed.

*Violation Consequence: ROBOT not allowed to compete.*

| Teams may purchase additional parts for prototyping or as spares, but extra components may not be used to increase the quantity included on the final ROBOT. This rule is intended to help keep the level of competition equal for all teams. |
| :---- |

**R12	ROBOTS may also be constructed using the following additional “non-REV Robotics” parts:**
A.	Fasteners. Teams may replace lost or damaged fasteners with commercially available parts, provided they are functionally identical (e.g., same material, strength, form, size, length) to those supplied in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts. The total number of fasteners on the final ROBOT must not exceed the quantity originally provided in the 2026 *FIRST* Global REV Robotics Kit of Parts. This rule allows teams to source local replacements, not to gain a strategic or construction advantage. During inspection, teams may be required to replace any non-compliant fasteners with legal ones before their ROBOT is allowed to compete.
B.	Replacement zip ties or cable ties. Must be functionally equivalent to REV Robotics zip ties in all ways (for example, may not be longer, stronger, or stiffer).
C.	Lubricants used only in small amounts to reduce friction within the ROBOT. Lubricants must not contaminate the PLAYING FIELD, GAME PIECES, or other ROBOTS. Use of lubricants is limited to:
i. 	Non-aerosol grease used in extreme moderation only on the internal gearing of motors or servos as needed after a gearing repair.
ii.	Bicycle chain lubricant for repairing rusty roller chain, provided it is used sparingly and excess amounts are wiped off.
D.	Small amounts of tape or heat-shrink tubing used solely for:
i. 	Labeling wires, motors, or sensors.
ii.	Securing wire connections.

# **6\. THE TOURNAMENT STRUCTURE** {#6.-the-tournament-structure}

## **6.1. Overview** {#6.1.-overview}

The 2026 *FIRST* Global Challenge is played in a tournament format, consisting of three types of MATCHES. RANKING MATCHES provide each team with the opportunity to compete with its ROBOT on the PLAYING FIELD to earn points that will help determine their rank amongst the total participating teams. PLAYOFF MATCHES follow the RANKING MATCHES to determine which teams advance to the FINAL MATCHES. FINAL MATCHES determine the *FIRST* Global Winning Alliance.

## **6.2. Practice Matches** {#6.2.-practice-matches}

Each team will have the opportunity to play in an unscored PRACTICE MATCH at the EVENT. PRACTICE MATCHES are held before the start of RANKING MATCHES to allow teams to test their ROBOTS. Practice fields are available throughout the duration of the EVENT for teams to continue testing their ROBOTS.

## **6.3. Ranking Matches** {#6.3.-ranking-matches}

Each team will play a set number of RANKING MATCHES based on the time allowed in the schedule. RANKING MATCH assignments and schedules will be generated once ROBOTS have been inspected. Teams will earn points based on their REGIONAL ALLIANCE’S performance during RANKING MATCHES, and the points will determine which teams advance to the PLAYOFF MATCHES.

Each team is ranked based on the average of the sum of their best MATCH scores. Each team’s single lowest-scoring MATCH will not be included in their RANKING score.

If one or more teams are tied for a ranking position at the conclusion of RANKING MATCHES, the next factor for determining which teams advance to the PLAYOFF MATCHES is the highest single RANKING MATCH score from the team’s RANKING MATCHES. The team with the highest single RANKING MATCH score advances.

If there continues to be a tie between one or more teams after ranking position and the highest single RANKING MATCH score from the team’s RANKING MATCHES has been considered, the next factor for determining which teams advance to the PLAYOFF MATCHES is total SUPPRESSION UNIT points. The team with the highest cumulative SUPPRESSION UNIT points from all RANKING MATCHES (not including the team’s lowest-scoring MATCH) advances.

## **6.4. Alliance Selection** {#6.4.-alliance-selection}

TOURNAMENT ALLIANCES for the PLAYOFF MATCHES and FINAL MATCHES are selected following the conclusion of RANKING MATCHES, and do not change for the remainder of the EVENT.

A total of 32 teams compete in PLAYOFF MATCHES, divided into eight four-team TOURNAMENT ALLIANCES. At the conclusion of RANKING MATCHES, the top 24 teams will be automatically placed into eight TOURNAMENT ALLIANCES based on their rank and according to Table 6-1. Each of the eight TOURNAMENT ALLIANCES is led by one of the top-ranked eight teams as determined at the conclusion of the RANKING MATCHES. An additional eight teams will be selected via a random draw from the remaining pool of teams to fill the fourth slot on each TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE. The random draw is selected from the remaining teams (ranked \#25 and below) and is independent of team rank.

Only three of the four TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE teams will compete in each PLAYOFF and FINAL MATCH. The TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE teams determine which ROBOTS will participate in each MATCH. Four teams per TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE allows for teams to make strategic choices as to which ROBOT plays each MATCH, while allowing for more teams to participate in the PLAYOFF and FINAL MATCHES. All teams on the TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE are treated equally and will all share in any awards that the team receives.

The FINAL MATCHES consist of three MATCHES. Each of the top three ranked TOURNAMENT ALLIANCES from the PLAYOFF MATCHES will play two MATCHES, one against each of the other two TOURNAMENT ALLIANCES, according to Table 6-3.

## **6.7. Tournament Rules** {#6.7.-tournament-rules}

**T01	All four teams in a TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE must participate in a minimum of one PLAYOFF MATCH.** If a TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE’S teams do not come to an agreement on which teams will play in each MATCH, a random selection will be made by *FIRST* Global.

**T02	Tournament rules apply to all four teams on each TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE in the PLAYOFF and FINAL MATCHES.**

**T03	*FIRST* Global reserves the right to allocate one or more teams to be included in the random draw teams.** This will only be exercised when justified by extenuating factors.

**T04	*FIRST* Global reserves the right to modify the pairing of teams based on situational considerations at the EVENT after the final rankings are determined.** In the event that these pairings must change, every effort will be made to ensure changes have minimal impact on the TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE average strength by keeping changes within three ranking positions. Teams will be notified of these changes prior to the TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE pairing announcements.

**T05	In the event of a disagreement within a TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE, the highest ranked team is the decider.** All teams in a TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE are encouraged to collaborate on MATCH strategy and REGIONAL ALLIANCE composition.

**T06	Teams may not save sections of spectator seating.** If team members are not present, seats should remain available for other teams and spectators.

## **6.8. Awards** {#6.8.-awards}

The following quantitative awards are determined solely by ROBOT performance in the *Igniting Innovation* Robotics Challenge.

* *FIRST* Global Grand Challenge Award \- Gold, silver, and bronze medals awarded to the teams with the highest RANKING score
* *FIRST* Global Winning Alliance \- Gold medals awarded to the four teams on the top-ranked TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE
* *FIRST* Global Finalist Alliances \- Silver medals awarded to the second-ranked TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE, and bronze medals awarded to the third-ranked TOURNAMENT ALLIANCE
